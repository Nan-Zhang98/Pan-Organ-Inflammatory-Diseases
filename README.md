# Pan-Organ Inflammatory Diseases

## Overview

Current research on inflammatory diseases largely remains confined to single organs, which limits our ability to elucidate the shared immunological principles underlying cross-organ inflammatory disorders. 
In this project, we constructed a comprehensive pan-organ single-cell transcriptomic atlas comprising over **3.87 million cells** across **8 human organs** and **29 inflammatory diseases**.
We identify IL1B⁺NLRP3⁺ macrophages as a highly conserved subset shared across diverse cross-tissue inflammatory diseases. IL1B⁺NLRP3⁺ macrophages possess potent pathogen-sensing capabilities to trigger innate immune cascades. Multidimensional trajectory inference reveals that IL1B⁺NLRP3⁺ macrophages undergo STAT3-mediated pathological rewiring and transition into SPP1⁺CCL2⁺ macrophage phenotype. The temporal progression of this state transition is further transcriptomically recapitulated in an in vivo murine pneumonia model. High-resolution spatial transcriptomics further shows that SPP1⁺CCL2⁺ macrophages actively disrupt epithelial barrier boundaries and direct targeted T-cell infiltration through the CCL2–CCR2 and CXCL12–CXCR4 signaling axes. 
Collectively, our study reveals a conserved macrophage pathogenic trajectory that connects innate immune activation with adaptive immune recruitment across human inflammatory diseases. These findings establish a unified conceptual framework for pan-organ chronic inflammatory diseases.
