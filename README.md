# Pan-Organ Inflammatory Diseases

## Overview

Current research on inflammatory diseases largely remains confined to single organs, which limits our ability to elucidate the shared immunological principles underlying cross-organ inflammatory disorders. In this project, we constructed a comprehensive pan-organ single-cell transcriptomic atlas comprising over **3.87 million cells** across **8 human organs** and **29 inflammatory diseases**.

Through systematic single-cell integration, macrophage subclustering, trajectory inference, regulatory network analysis, in vivo validation, and spatial transcriptomic analysis, we identified a conserved macrophage pathogenic trajectory that links innate immune activation with adaptive immune recruitment across human inflammatory diseases.

## Key Findings

- Integrated a large-scale pan-organ single-cell atlas covering more than **3.87 million cells** from diverse inflammatory disease contexts.
- Identified **IL1B⁺NLRP3⁺ macrophages** as a highly conserved inflammatory macrophage subset shared across multiple organs and diseases.
- Found that IL1B⁺NLRP3⁺ macrophages exhibit strong pathogen- and damage-sensing capacity and activate innate inflammatory cascades.
- Revealed a pathological transition from **IL1B⁺NLRP3⁺ macrophages** toward **SPP1⁺CCL2⁺ macrophages**, accompanied by STAT3-associated regulatory rewiring.
- Recapitulated this macrophage state transition in an in vivo murine pneumonia model.
- Demonstrated through high-resolution spatial transcriptomics that SPP1⁺CCL2⁺ macrophages may contribute to epithelial barrier disruption and targeted T-cell infiltration.
- Highlighted the potential involvement of the **CCL2–CCR2** and **CXCL12–CXCR4** signaling axes in inflammatory niche remodeling.
